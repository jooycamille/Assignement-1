More on Voice Technology

Purpose

Voice Technology, also known as Voice Search or Voice Recognition Software are programs that can recognise and record audible commands of the human language and translate them into code i.e. computer language, that can interact with other programs. [1] [2] [3]

Uses:

  • Transcription and Dictation
  • Voice Assistances
  • Automatic Command and Control
  • Social Media Messengers
  • Customer Experience
Advantages Disadvantages
Useful for those with disabilities (especially for the eyes and limbs) Expensive, since they usually come as a function of smart devices
Can be connected to other devices and smart appliances, which helps to complete tasks more easily and efficiently Inaccuracy in recognising voice
Conveniency Many tasks cannot be done through voice technology due to the complexity of the programming involved

Development

The first inkling of voice technology came about through the efforts of Thomas Edison. He created the device from a combination of his previous inventions: telephone and telegraph. The thought that he could record telephone messages, similar to how telegraphic messages and words are recorded through indentations on paper is what sparked him to create the first device that could record sound and play it back. This decide was created on 4th December, 1877.

Next came the Voder, the first speech synthesiser, a machine created to mimic how humans talk, which was invented by A team of engineers at Bell Labs, led by Homer Dudley in 1936 as an attempt to further communication technology. The first real voice recognition software was also made by Bell Labs, who created Audrey, a program capable of recognising the digits from 0 to 9 with 90% accuracy. [4] [5]

Growth and Future

Voice technology has come a long way developing to the adaptations of ever changing lifestyles of people. It was introduced by Thomas Edison into the world and made its first evolution in 1950, when Bell Labs invented Voder, then Audrey, an automatic digit recognition machine. This was then enhanced overtime with more vocabularies recognised either by speech patterns, fixed templates, or sounds and pitch. It reached a major breakthrough in 2010 where Google invented an application called Google Voice Search. With this advancement, it paved the way for Siri whose success only pushed the voice technology to shoot up. Because of this, companies now created their own digital assistants we now widely use in our everyday life such as Siri, Amazon Alexa, Cortana, Google, Bixby, etc. Integrations of voice assistants into our daily gadgets i.e. mobile phones, pushed more devices that are used in different settings i.e. home, hospitals, schools, cars, businesses, and more. Since more consumers are leaning towards the digital world, indsutries are now widely adopting this era, where voice technology benefits businesses' capability to generate revenue, improve efficiency and increase productivity. According to the Industry Report: Trends and Predictions for Voice Technology in 2021 by Speechmatics, 68% of respondents currently have voice strategy in place in their company compared with 50% in 2020. Those who doesn't have voice strategy, 60% said that it is something they will consider in the next 5 years. With the pandemic COVID-19 hitting globally, companies are more inclines to shift into voice technology, where many industries like media and entertaiinment, banking, financial services, insurance, healthcare and life sciences, education, government, etc have proven that voice technology's capabilities are limitless, and there is no stopping it.
